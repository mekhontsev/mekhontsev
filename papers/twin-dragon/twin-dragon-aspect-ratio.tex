\begin{remark}\label{rem:rotation-weights}
The formula extends to IFS with arbitrary unit-modulus weights,
\[
  f_k(z) = \varepsilon_k\, a\,(z + t_k), \qquad \varepsilon_k \in \mathbb{C},\quad |\varepsilon_k|=1,
\]
provided the centering condition \(\sum_{k=1}^N \varepsilon_k t_k = 0\) holds
(which implies \(E[Z]=0\) by the fixed-point equation for \(h(z)=z\)).
Define the \emph{weighted algebraic variance}
\[
  \tau_\varepsilon := \frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N \varepsilon_k^2\, t_k^2 \in \mathbb{C},
  \qquad
  \alpha_2 := \frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^N \varepsilon_k^2 \in \mathbb{C},
\]
and the \emph{weighted digit anisotropy}\n\[\kappa_\varepsilon := |\tau_\varepsilon|/\sigma^2 \in [0,1].\]
Applying the fixed-point equation to \(h(z)=z^2\) gives
\[
  E[Z^2] = a^2\alpha_2\, E[Z^2] + a^2\tau_\varepsilon,
  \qquad\text{so}\qquad
  E[Z^2] = \frac{a^2\tau_\varepsilon}{1 - a^2\alpha_2}.
\]
Since \(|\varepsilon_k|^2=1\), the equation for \(h(z)=|z|^2\) is unchanged:
$E[|Z|^2] = |a|^2\sigma^2/(1-|a|^2)$.  
The ellipticity ratio is therefore
\[
  \rho
  = \frac{|E[Z^2]|}{E[|Z|^2]}
  = \frac{(1-|a|^2)\,\kappa_\varepsilon}{|1 - a^2\alpha_2|},
\]
and the aspect ratio formula becomes
\begin{equation}\label{eq:family-rot}
  \mathrm{AR}^2
  = \frac{|1-a^2\alpha_2| - (1-|a|^2)\,\kappa_\varepsilon}
         {|1-a^2\alpha_2| + (1-|a|^2)\,\kappa_\varepsilon}.
\end{equation}
This reduces to~\eqref{eq:family} when \(\varepsilon_k=\pm 1\)
(so \(\alpha_2=1\) and \(\kappa_\varepsilon=\kappa\)).
When all weights are equal, \(\varepsilon_k = e^{i\theta}\),
one has \(\alpha_2 = e^{2i\theta}\) and \(\tau_\varepsilon = e^{2i\theta}\tau\),
so \(\kappa_\varepsilon = \kappa\) and~\eqref{eq:family-rot} describes
the same attractor rotated by~\(\theta\), with AR unchanged.
When the \(\varepsilon_k\) are the \(N\)-th roots of unity (\(N\geq 3\)),
\(\alpha_2 = 0\) and~\eqref{eq:family-rot} reduces to
\(\mathrm{AR}^2 = (1-(1-|a|^2)\kappa_\varepsilon)/(1+(1-|a|^2)\kappa_\varepsilon)\).

In particular, this framework covers self-similar tiles whose pieces
are related by rotations: if the \(k\)-th piece is rotated by angle \(\theta_k\)
relative to the whole, setting \(\varepsilon_k = e^{i\theta_k}\) and
verifying the centering condition \(\sum e^{i\theta_k}t_k=0\)
brings such IFS within the scope of~\eqref{eq:family-rot}.
The full classification of aspect ratios for rotation-weighted tiles
over \(\mathbb{Z}[i]\) is a natural direction for future work.
\end{remark}
