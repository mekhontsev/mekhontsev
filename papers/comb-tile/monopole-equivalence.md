# Monopole Equivalence Theorem (Draft)

## Setup

Let $\{A_n\}_{n \geq n_0}$ be a family of disk-like self-similar tiles in $\mathbb{R}^2$, each generated by $n$ contractions of ratio $1/\sqrt{n}$, satisfying:

- the number of edge neighbors $|\mathcal{A}| \leq k$ for all $n$ (bounded neighbor complexity),
- $\dim_H(\partial A_n) \to 2$ as $n \to \infty$,
- the boundary substitution matrix $M_n$ is primitive for all sufficiently large $n$.

Let $d$ denote the number of nonzero eigenvalues of $M_n$, and define

$$c \;=\; \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\operatorname{tr}(M_n)}{n}.$$  

## Main Result

**Theorem (Monopole Equivalence).** Under the above hypotheses, $c$ is a positive integer with $1 \leq c \leq k$, and the following are equivalent:

**(i)** The boundary GIFS has monopole structure: $\rho_n = |\lambda_2|/\lambda_1 \to 0$.

**(ii)** Exactly one boundary piece has a self-similar diagonal of order $\Theta(n)$: $c = 1$.

Moreover:

### Case $c = 1$ (monopole regime)

$$\rho_n = O\!(n^{-1/(d-1)}) \longrightarrow 0$$

$$2 - \dim_H(\partial A_n) = O\!\left(\frac{1}{n^2 \log n}\right)$$

The Perron eigenvector concentrates on a single boundary piece: one piece carries $(1 - O(1/n))$ of the total $\mathcal{H}^s$-measure of $\partial A_n$.

### Case $c \geq 2$ (competing regime)

$$\rho_n \;\geq\; \frac{c-1}{c} + o(1) \;>\; 0$$

$$2 - \dim_H(\partial A_n) = \Theta\!\left(\frac{1}{n \log n}\right)$$

Multiple boundary pieces carry comparable $\mathcal{H}^s$-measure; the spectral gap does not close, and the convergence $\dim_H \to 2$ is slower by a factor of $n$.

---

## Proof sketch

### $(ii) \Rightarrow (i)$

If $c = 1$ then $\operatorname{tr}(M_n) = n + O(1)$ and $\lambda_1 = n - O(1/n)$, so $\sum_{i \geq 2} \lambda_i = O(1)$. The product of all eigenvalues equals $\det(\text{nonzero block}) = O(n^{d-1})$, giving $\prod_{i \geq 2}|\lambda_i| = O(n^{d-2})$. Therefore

$$|\lambda_2|^{d-1} \leq \prod_{i \geq 2}|\lambda_i| = O(n^{d-2}),$$

hence $|\lambda_2| = O(n^{(d-2)/(d-1)})$ and $\rho_n = O(n^{-1/(d-1)}) \to 0$.

### $(i) \Rightarrow (ii)$

If $\rho_n \to 0$ then $|\lambda_i| = o(n)$ for all $i \geq 2$, so

$$\lambda_1 = \operatorname{tr}(M_n) - \sum_{i \geq 2}\lambda_i = cn + o(n).$$

Since $\lambda_1 < n$ (because $\dim_H(\partial A_n) < 2$), we get $c \leq 1$.

If $c = 0$ then $\sum_{i \geq 2}\lambda_i = -n + O(1)$ and $|\lambda_2| \geq n/(d-1)$, contradicting $\rho_n \to 0$.

Hence $c = 1$. $\square$

### Convergence rates

For $c = 1$: $n - \lambda_1 = O(1/n)$, giving $2 - s = O(1/(n^2\log n))$.

For $c \geq 2$: $\lambda_1 + \lambda_2 + \cdots = cn + O(1)$ with $\lambda_1 < n$ forces $n - \lambda_1 = \Theta(1)$, giving $2 - s = \Theta(1/(n\log n))$.

---

## Corollary (optimality of monopole)

Among all disk-like families with bounded neighbor complexity and $\dim_H(\partial A_n) \to 2$, the monopole regime $c = 1$ achieves the **fastest** convergence rate. The comb family, with $c = 1$ and $\rho_n \sim 1/\sqrt{n}$, is in this optimal class.

---

## Remark (for the comb family)

For the comb family, $c = 1$ because the unique self-similar diagonal is $(M_n)_{66} = n - 1$ (the boundary piece $B_6$ contains $n - 1$ scaled copies of itself). The actual spectral gap $\rho_n = 1/\sqrt{n} + O(n^{-3/2})$ is sharper than the general bound $O(n^{-1/3})$ (since $d = 4$), due to the factorization of the subsidiary cubic into $(t+1)(t^2 - (n-2))$.

---

## Model example ($c = 2$, competing regime)

Consider the $4 \times 4$ primitive matrix

$$M_n = \begin{pmatrix} n-1 & 0 & 1 & 1 \\ 0 & n-1 & 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$$

with $\operatorname{tr}(M_n) = 2(n-1)$, so $c = 2$. By symmetry the eigenvalues split into a symmetric sector (eigenvector $(1,1,x,x)$) with $\lambda^2 - (n-1)\lambda - 4 = 0$, giving $\lambda_1 = n - 1 + 4/n + O(1/n^2)$, and an antisymmetric sector (eigenvector $(1,-1,0,0)$) with $\lambda_2 = n - 1$ exactly. Therefore

$$\rho_n = \frac{n-1}{n - 1 + 4/n} = 1 - \frac{4}{n(n-1)} \;\longrightarrow\; 1,$$

confirming no monopole structure, and

$$2 - \dim_H \;\sim\; \frac{2}{n \log n},$$

which is slower than the comb family's $8/(n^2 \log n)$ by a factor of $n/4$.

---

## Open Question

Does there exist a disk-like family with bounded neighbor complexity, $\dim_H(\partial A_n) \to 2$, and $c \geq 2$?

By the theorem above, such a family would necessarily have:
- $\rho_n \not\to 0$ (no monopole structure),
- slower convergence $2 - \dim_H = \Theta(1/(n\log n))$,
- multiple boundary pieces carrying comparable $\mathcal{H}^s$-measure.
